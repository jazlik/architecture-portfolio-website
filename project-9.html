STUDENT CENTER

type: Academic project

location: Warsaw, Poland

date: 2020

The project presents the Southern Student Centre for students of the Warsaw University of Technology. The idea is to implement a building into the current plot and propose a place of meeting for the students.

The plot is located in the south downtown of the city of Warsaw, glued to the public square in front of the Faculty of Material Engineering. This area is greatly surrounded by the greenery, parks, urban gardens and family garden allotments.

From the north and the west, the project is surrounded by parks and garden allotments. In the north, the gardens reach the Park Pole Mokotowskie, while in the west, they run in the Military Mausoleum Park. The location of the project between these parts forced the creation of a kind of gate between the two areas, making the building passable on the ground floor. It means that going through the student dormitory, any passer-by that wants to move from one green area to another, can pass through the atriums of the building.

Building's mass was created in ten main steps, shaping the final outcome of the building. The major influencing factor was the slanted shape of the plot, resulting in the division of the building and its extension.

The programme of the project assumes a building that beside taking something from the city, is also going to give something back. In that manner, there is a facade rain-water harvesting system which is collecting the water and storing it in the watertank on the underground level. Additionally, there is a greenhouse at the rooftop of the building, allowing students to grow their own plants and vegetables. This activity promotes the sustainable self-suficient food unit, that is able to satisfy its residents needs. Furthermore, beside its feeding function, greenhouse on the rooftop is going to fortife the bonds between the fellow inhabitants of the building.

The building's elevation is composed of the vertical bronze panels, to increase to area of the harvesting surface, but at the same time keep the exterior wall flat, as it is the best to collect the rainwater and then gather it in the underground watertank. In the place of windows, there are bronze shutter panels which are being controlled from the inside of the building, creating constantly changing composition of the building's elevation.
